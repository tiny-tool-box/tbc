I. Introduction

The mission (>5 years): create a model for fictional/speculative science such that: - it creates a parallel, in the natural sciences, to what already exists in the form of thought experiments, design fictions, and other non-empirical work in humanities and social sciences - bridges rather than divides the different disciplines (and we wou - enables citizen science engagement not just with constrained data collection and analysis efforts, but with the critical process of reading and building on existing scientific work Core Values: - revel in potentially absurd levels of multi-disciplinarity - all scientific products are free & all artist contributions are recognized and compensated - constructive artistic collaborations can be a valid, illuminating locus of synthesis of existing scientific knowledge The dream (>10 years): - build on models fo apprenticeship and peer-review in a more proactively-inclusive model - improve scientific literacy and communication of science to the public by improving the way in which "the public" could communicate back to science - build legitimacy for the scientific work done in a peer-produced process that would involve both scientific and non-scientific contributors, i.e., by establishing an open-access journal on speculative natural science The roadmap (next 2-3 years): reveal, part by part, the first universe within which ideas in the space of “fictional/speculative science” can be piloted, revised, experimented upon - Focus: collaborative production of narratives and multi-media experiences - as a way to build engagement/community, and also to spark interest in engaging with the imagined Dweller universe - Lost mail: laboratory for analog/digital material production (2018-2020) - The Dweller Universe graphic novel - a visual narrative set in the imagined world (2018-2021) - The Magazine: a pilot run for the future-imagined journal, but more following the pop-science magazine model for broader acceptability (2019-2021)
